# Long-Form Sales Letter Writing

Write billion-dollar proven sales letters using frameworks from the masters of direct response copywriting.

## Core Principle

Copy cannot create desire—it channels existing desires toward your product. Research the market's hopes, dreams, fears, and existing beliefs BEFORE writing.

## Quick Start Workflow

### 1. Research (60-80% of time)
Gather 7x more information than needed:
- **BDF Analysis**: Beliefs, Desires, Fears of the prospect
- **Voice of Customer**: Amazon reviews, forums, Reddit, customer conversations
- **Competitive Analysis**: Study existing promotions in the space
- **Awareness Level**: Determine where prospects fall (see [leads.md](references/leads.md))

### 2. Strategic Foundation
Before writing, define:
- **Big Idea**: One intellectually interesting AND emotionally compelling concept that leads to an inevitable purchase conclusion
- **Unique Mechanism**: The proprietary system/method/component that delivers results
- **Primary Promise**: The single biggest benefit
- **Target Awareness**: Match lead type to audience awareness level

### 3. Structure Selection
Choose primary framework based on copy length and audience:
- **AIDA**: Attention → Interest → Desire → Action (elaborate campaigns)
- **PAS**: Problem → Agitate → Solution (emotion-driven, shorter pieces)  
- **4 P's**: Promise → Picture → Proof → Push (proof-heavy needs)

See [frameworks.md](references/frameworks.md) for detailed breakdowns.

### 4. Write the Letter

**The Four-Legged Stool** (every promotion needs all four):
1. **Big/Unifying Idea** — One emotionally compelling concept
2. **Substantial Promise** — Clear benefit with supporting claims
3. **Ample Proof** — Evidence for every claim (see [proof.md](references/proof.md))
4. **Loads of Credibility** — For product, expert, and company

**Rule of One** for blockbuster promotions:
- One good idea
- One core emotion
- One captivating story
- One single, desirable benefit
- One inevitable response

### 5. Create the Slippery Slide
Every element exists to get readers to the next element:
- Headline → First sentence → Second sentence → CTA
- Use "seeds of curiosity" transitions: "But there's more..." "Now here's the best part..."
- Short first sentences that demand continuation
- Open loops that must be closed

### 6. Close Construction
- **VVU Formula**: Verb + Value + Urgency ("Get Your [Product] Today Before Midnight")
- Multiple CTAs placed after key benefit sections
- Risk reversal through guarantees (longer = fewer refunds)
- P.S. restates key benefit and deadline

## Quality Checklist

Before delivery, verify:
- [ ] Single Big Idea that leads to inevitable purchase conclusion
- [ ] Four-Legged Stool complete (Idea, Promise, Proof, Credibility)
- [ ] Lead type matches audience awareness level
- [ ] Golden Thread maintained (emotional connection never goes slack)
- [ ] All claims have supporting proof
- [ ] Clear, urgent CTA with risk reversal
- [ ] Passes CUB test: Not Confusing, Unbelievable, or Boring
